Peter Gabriel

The man who inspires millions

  • 1950 - Born in Chobham, England
  • 1965 - Formed Garden Wall with Tony Banks on piano and Chris Stewart on drums. Peter and Banks bonded over music and started to write songs.
  • 1967 - Garden Wall disbanded, Gabriel and Banks met Anthony Philips and Mike Rutherford to work on a demo tape of songs. The tape was received by Jonathan Kingn from Charterhouse and signed the group inmediately after hearing Gabriel's voice.
  • 1968 - First studio album, 'From Genesis to Revelation' released, but was a commercial failure.
  • 1970 - Due to the commercial failure of the first album the band decided to not make Genesis a full-time working band.
  • 1970 - Genesis releases their second album 'Trespass' and Anthony Phlilips leaves the band.
  • 1970 - Steve Hackett joined the group. No long after the group's drummer was replaced by 19-year-old Phil Collins.
  • 1971 - Peter Gabriel and Jill Moore married. And the band releases their third album 'Nursery Cryme'.
  • 1973 - Genesis recorded their most succesful album to date, 'Selling England by the Pound'.
  • 1974 - The Lamb Lies Down on Brodway releases. The band decided to undertook a highly profitable North American tour.
  • 1975 - Due to family circunstances, Gabriel left the band.
  • 1977 - Peter after some preparation return to the music scene as a solo Artist. His first solo album, titled 'Peter Gabriel' is released in 1977
  • 1977-Present - Peter Gabriel collaborated with musicians from all around the world. He created WOMAD 'World of Music, Arts and Dance' movement. Created the Real World Studios and record label to facillitate the creation and distribution of music by various artist. He has a longstanding interest in human rights, helping with carity and technology. And he conitinues to create his music to this day.
